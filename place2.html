Lying on the western coast, Goa is India's smallest state and unlike any other, known for its endless beaches, stellar nightlife, eclectic seafood, world-heritage listed architecture. Spread across just 3,702 km, Goa lies in the Konkan region. One of the only few destinations that is open 24x7. The laid-backness (susegad) of Goa attracts as many international tourists as it does Indians, or even more so.

PLACES TO VISIT

1. Calangute Beach
Situated 15 km from Panjim, Calangute Beach is the longest beach in North Goa, stretching from Candolim to Baga. Due to its sheer size and popularity, it is a hub for tourists and backpackers from all over the world.
2. Fort Aguada, Goa Overview
Fort Aguada is a 17th-century Portuguese fort looking out at the confluence of Mandovi River and the Arabian Sea. The crumbling ramparts of the fort stand on the Sinquerim Beach, approximately 18 km from Panjim. The highlight of the fort is a lone four-storey lighthouse (which is one-of-its-kind in Asia )and a stunning view of the sunset.
3. Water Sports in Goa, Goa Overview
Water Sports in Goa have always seen its fare share of excited tourists and adventure seekers. The beach capital of India enjoys a long and uninterrupted coastline with relatively calmer waves and even sea floors. While water sports in Goa can be enjoyed at any one of the beaches in the north or the south, they are more popular and draw huge crowds at Baga, Calangute, Benaulim, Candolim, and nearby beaches

Local transport in Goa

Bus : Bus connectivity is good for popular areas of Goa and fares are low (ranging from INR 5 to INR 40) and still gives you a great view of the city

Scooty, Bike and Car : Since there are no Ola and Uber services available in Goa, you could rent a motorcycle, scooter or a car if you have a valid license. Riding on bikes is the most popular way of commuting in Goa for young tourists, including foreigners.

From the Airport : The most popular and convenient mode of travelling from Dabolim Airport to any part of Goa is the prepaid taxis
